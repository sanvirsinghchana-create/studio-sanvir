About

Sanvir Chana is a filmmaker drawn to honest stories and quiet moments. His work is rooted in observation rather than direction, creating films that feel timeless, intimate and deeply human.

Every film is made entirely by hand, with no templates, no formulas, and no two ever the same.

He shoots exclusively on Kodak motion picture film, embracing the texture, warmth and character that only analogue can offer. Film demands patience, intention and trust. There are no unlimited takes, only moments that matter.

Each film is scored with an original composition, written specifically for the people at its heart. Rather than relying on licensed music, every piece is composed from scratch, creating something that belongs entirely to that story. A piece of music that will live alongside the memories it accompanies for years to come.

Whether documenting a wedding, a family, or a place, Sanvir's aim is always the same: to create films that feel truthful today and even more meaningful decades from now.

Featured Film

Wedding Film Memories of Tuscany

Sam & Olivia

Tuscany, Italy · Kodak 16mm

Scroll

Some weddings you photograph. A few you sit inside, and let happen.

Sam and Olivia were married over two unhurried days in the Tuscan hills, all of it shot on 16mm — three rolls a day, never more. The format asks a certain patience of you: you stop chasing every moment and begin waiting for the right one.

There is something Kodak film does that nothing else will. It flatters no one and forgives everyone — the afternoon light came through the grain like honey and held its warmth into the last of the evening.

There are no second takes. The crank turns, the magazine empties, and whatever happened in those thirty seconds is the only version there will ever be. The film is as singular as the day it remembers.

Marcus Herne scored it afterward — a piece written for the two of them alone. We finished as the music found the picture, the courtyard full, Sam and Olivia somewhere in the middle of it.

— Sanvir Chana
